Are you the real brave?

April 21, 2020
1 min
Albert John Tulop

When I was a kid, I always took the concept of being brave as the trait of always being ready for a fistfight. I thought that it is the only way for someone to be called brave. Well, maybe it is my nature as a little boy. I always have this little admiration for those kids who were brave. I mean, they have something special, right?

As I grew up, it did not changed so much. It is that same bravery, except by this time, enemies were bigger, and sometimes literally. I didn’t really gave much thought about it.

Time passes and different kinds of experiences were being thrown at me. Happiness, love, frustrations, taking risks, break downs, achievements, and many other things. I always thought that this is just how life is, but what I didn’t realized is that through those experiences, that bravery that I knew was being changed little by little until the time that it was completely new.

Life made me realize what the real bravery means.


"Bravery is trusting God, even when you have no idea what is going to happen."

"Bravery is continuing to worship, even when you’re in hardships."

"Bravery is giving it all, even if you’re unsure of the outcome."

"Bravery is standing up again, even if you experienced failure countless of times."

"Bravery is believing that God is with you, even in the times that you don’t feel it."


Bravery is surrendering to God. With or without those uncertainties. Win or lose. Acknowledging that we are only humans, that we are vulnerable, that we are not impenetrable, and that we need God in our lives.

May we be brave to be this brave.

When trial and testing come, are you willing to be brave?




“Not only so, but we also glory in our sufferings, because we know that suffering produces perseverance; perseverance, character; and character, hope. And hope does not put us to shame, because God’s love has been poured out into our hearts through the Holy Spirit, who has been given to us.”

Romans 5:3-5
